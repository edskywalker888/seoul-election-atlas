# Rebuilding Korea Party (조국혁신당, Cho Kuk Innovation Party) — English Wikipedia (captured)

## Basics
- Founded: 2024-03-03
- Founder: Cho Kuk (former Justice Minister under Moon administration)
- Korean name: 조국혁신당 (Joguk Hyeoksin-dang) — "Homeland Innovation Party," wordplay on Cho's given name
- Position: Centre-left, "more progressive than the Democratic Party"

## 2024 election performance
- **PR vote share: 24.25% (6,874,278 votes)**
- **PR seats won: 12**
- **Constituency candidates fielded: 0** (deliberate strategy to avoid splitting the anti-Yoon vote with DPK)
- 3rd-largest party in 300-seat National Assembly (after DPK 175, PPP 108)

## Strategy and platform
- "Lame duck, then dead duck" rhetoric targeting Yoon
- Primary goal: abolish "prosecutorial dictatorship"; depoliticize Public Prosecutor's Office
- Greater government intervention; expanded social safety net
- "Peaceful relationship of cooperation with North Korea and denuclearization"

## Cho Kuk's legal context
- Dec 2024 (post-election): Supreme Court upheld 2-year prison sentence for document falsification
- Lost National Assembly seat; barred from running for office until 2031
- 2025: Endorsed Lee Jae-myung; did not field own presidential candidate

## Strategic significance for 2024
By running ONLY proportional candidates, Cho Kuk Innovation captured a quarter of the PR vote without competing with DPK in any constituency — extracting maximum opposition seats from the same anti-Yoon mood.
