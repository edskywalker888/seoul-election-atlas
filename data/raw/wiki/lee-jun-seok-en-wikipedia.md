# Lee Jun-seok (이준석) — English Wikipedia (captured)

## PPP leadership and exit
- 2021-06-11: Elected leader of People Power Party — youngest in Korean conservative history
- Under his leadership: PPP won 2022 presidential, 12/17 mayoral/gubernatorial in June 2022 local elections
- 2022-07-08: PPP ethics commission suspended Lee for 6 months (sexual-favors and evidence-destruction allegations)
- 2022-08-09: Removed from leadership; suspension extended Oct 2022
- 2023-11-02: Suspension formally removed
- 2023-12-27: Announced departure from PPP, declaring formation of new party

## Reform Party (개혁신당) founding
- 2024-01-20: Officially founded
- 2024 National Assembly election: Lee won **Hwaseong B with 42.41%** (51,856 votes)
- "Unexpected" given he moved to the constituency only weeks before the election

## Voter base / appeal
- Meritocracy rhetoric, opposes affirmative action and quotas
- Anti-feminist positions; strong support from *idaenam* (이대남, men in 20s-30s)
- Criticism of "political correctness" and "faux feminism"

## Election relevance
Reform Party won 3 NA seats total in 2024 (per wiki-22-en). Represents a center-right break from PPP that captured the *idaenam* young-male demographic — a different fragmentation vector than the Cho Kuk Innovation Party's progressive PR push.
