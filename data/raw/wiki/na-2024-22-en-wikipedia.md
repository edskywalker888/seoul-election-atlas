# 2024 South Korean Legislative Election — English Wikipedia (captured summary)

## Election basics
- Date: 2024-04-10
- Turnout: 66.97% (constituency), 66.99% (proportional) — +0.77–0.78pp vs 2020
- Highest legislative turnout since 1992

## Seat outcomes (national)
- Democratic Party + Democratic Alliance: 173
- People Power Party + People Future Party: 108
- Rebuilding Korea Party: 12
- Reform Party: 3

## Major drivers

### Government performance & scandals
- Election framed as "mid-term evaluation" of President Yoon Suk-yeol's administration
- Presidential family controversies (president's wife, former minister)
- Opposition leader trials: Lee Jae-myung (bribery), Cho Kuk (forgery)

### Economic hardship
- "Rising inflation and the ongoing medical crisis" shaped sentiment
- Ruling party framing: "uncooperative National Assembly"
- Opposition framing: Yoon administration "incompetent," causing "socioeconomic downturn"

### Green Onion Incident (2024-03-18)
- Yoon visited grocery store; described 875-won (~$0.65) green onion price as "reasonable"
- Real price "three to four times higher"
- Opposition exploited; #greenonions875won trended
- NEC banned green onions at polling stations — widely ridiculed

### Lee Jae-myung assassination attempt
- 2024-01-02, Gadeokdo airport construction site, Busan
- Stabbing; Lee survived, campaigned through recovery

## Seoul-specific
- Democratic Party: 37 seats in Seoul
- People Power Party: 11 seats in Seoul
- Total constituency seats in Seoul: 48
- Democratic took central Seoul (Jung-Seongdong, Yongsan area, Gwangjin, Dongdaemun, Jungnang, Seongbuk, Gangbuk, western districts) + Jongno (gain from PPP)
- PPP retained: Yongsan, Seocho 갑/을, Gangnam 갑/을/병, Songpa 갑/을, Dongjak 을

## Electoral system
- Satellite parties to exploit PR seats:
  - Democratic → Democratic Alliance of Korea
  - PPP → People Future Party

## Analytical claims
- PPP self-assessment: Yoon administration unable to push reform agenda due to opposition-controlled NA
- Democratic framing: administration "incompetent"
- Rebuilding Korea: pledged to make Yoon "first a lame duck, then a dead duck"
- Exit polls: Democratic alliance 168–197 seats vs ruling coalition 85–111
