# Death of Chae Su-geun (Corporal Chae / 채상병) — English Wikipedia (captured)

## Note on naming
- 채상병 (Chae Sang-byeol) is the rank-based name used in Korean political discourse: 상병 = Corporal
- Personal name: Chae Su-geun
- Same person; treated as the "Chae Sang-byeol incident" in Korean coverage

## Basics
- Date of death: 2023-07-19
- Location: Yecheon County during 2023 South Korea floods rescue operation
- Cause: Reassigned from ground searches to underwater work without proper safety equipment (no life jacket); swept away

## The investigation-pressure (수사외압) controversy
- Defense Ministry initially approved Marine Corps investigation
- Then **halted handover to police** and charged investigation head with insubordination
- Revised report reduced accused parties from 8 to 2
- Direct quote: "Despite initially approving the investigations findings, Minister of Defence Lee Jong-sup subsequently ordered that the handover to the police be halted."

## Political fallout timeline
- 2023-07-19: Death
- 2023-07–08: Investigation controversy emerges
- 2024-03: Lee Jong-sup appointed ambassador to Australia
- 2024-03 (within weeks): Lee resigns ambassadorship due to ongoing controversy
- 2024-09: Parliament's Legislation and Judiciary Committee passes special-counsel probe bill (Yoon had previously vetoed similar bills)

## Polling impact
Article notes the death "damaged the public approval of the presidency of Yoon Suk Yeol" but provides no specific polling figures. wiki-22-ko cites the Chae case as one of the named governance-controversy drivers of the 22nd election.
